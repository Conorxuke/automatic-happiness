Crib sheet · Airia call · back-office orchestration

What to ask Airia — and why.

Concerns down the left, the questions to fire next to each. Built to keep open during the call.

The one thing to hold onto

The risk isn't Airia — it's the build around it. Airia covers the four engines, routing and governance well. The dashboard, the Supabase/SharePoint schema, and the human-in-the-loop queue are bespoke — exactly what a thin tech team can't easily build or maintain. Spend the call finding out how much of that Airia can absorb, so we own as little custom code as possible.

Token vs seat pricing

Their published model is execution-based with free seats — so the framing is really executions + model tokens, not seats.
What counts as one execution — a full item end-to-end, or each engine call? (Extract→Draft→Audit could be 3+.)
Are model tokens bundled, or bring-your-own-key billed separately?
Does cost scale with item-volume or headcount — and what tier are we actually on a year in?

Technical overhead

How much is config vs. real engineering — especially the connective tissue, not the engines.
Can all four engines be built in Agent Builder without an engineer?
Native connectors for Teams, SharePoint, Asana — which are out of the box?
Can human-in-the-loop approval live inside an Airia workflow natively, so we don't build a custom queue?

Manageability

Can a non-technical coordinator actually run and extend this without us calling for help each time.
Can a non-technical coordinator add a new "topping" (config) on their own?
When an engine misbehaves, who debugs — us or you?
Is provenance (engine, model version, timestamp) captured natively?

Running on low internal tech capacity — the ask

We've lost internal depth. The goal is to push surface onto Airia as managed, and keep owned code minimal.
What can you take off our plate — managed setup, professional services, or a partner who builds it?
How much of the dashboard + Supabase glue can Airia absorb so we maintain less code?
Strategic flag · the Medtronic thesis

Enterprise audit ≠ GxP validation. The "same build we'd sell to Medtronic" logic rests on DHF / 21 CFR Part 11 / computer-system validation — a higher bar than audit logs. Ask directly whether Airia supports it; the answer validates or undercuts the whole resale case.

Strategic flag · stay portable

Concentration risk on a 2024 startup is real — but we've hedged it. Keep the system of record and live state in our own Supabase/SharePoint, outside Airia, so the engines stay replaceable even if Airia later isn't the right home.
